Location Based Services

A location-based service (LBS) is a software application for mobile device that requires knowledge about where the mobile device is located. Location-based services are query-based and provide the end user with useful information such as "Where is the nearest ATM?" or other marketing information to customers who are in a specific geographical area.

A Location Based System requires five basic components: the service provider's software application, a mobile network to transmit data and requests for service, a content provider to supply the end user with geo-specific information, a positioning component (see GPS) and the end user's mobile device. By law, location-based services must be permission-based. That means that the end user must opt-in to the service in order to use it.

Although location-based services have been around since 2000, they have mostly been used in commerce with a subscription-based business model. The release of Apple's 3G iPhone and Google's LBS-enabled Android operating system, however, has allowed developers to introduce millions of consumers to LBS.
